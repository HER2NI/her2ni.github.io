HER2NI: Human–Emergent Resonance to Neural Intelligence

HER2NI is a protocol for representing, encoding, and exchanging coherence metrics between humans and AI systems. It defines model-agnostic metrics for interaction stability and provides a foundation for safety-aligned human–AI collaboration.

Current Focus: Coherence Geometry in Human–AI Interaction

Current research examines how HER2NI coherence metrics detect and characterise the geometric structure of stability, drift, and contradiction in complex, recursive dialogues. By mapping these dynamics in real time, we aim to guide and stabilise both human and AI behaviour, enabling safer and more interpretable multi-turn interactions. The objective is to establish reliable operational regimes for LLM systems functioning under high cognitive or emotional load, improving safety, consistency, and the effectiveness of modulation frameworks used in adaptive dialogue.

Active Lines of Inquiry
  • Detecting early cognitive drift and interaction collapse using Hs(t) curves.
  • Characterising cross-linguistic distress markers and ambiguous metaphorical language.
  • Evaluating tone, pacing, and abstraction adjustments for non-clinical support scenarios.
  • Providing metrics & tools to complement existing AI safety work.
HER2NI Metrics

HER2NI introduces three core metrics for cross-substrate cognitive alignment:

  • Cs — Carbon Score: human cognitive coherence and interaction stability.
  • Ss — Silicon Score: model-side processing load and reasoning intensity.
  • Hs — HER Score: emergent resonance between human and AI states over time.

Together, these metrics enable early detection of instability and provide a structured basis for aligning AI behaviour with human cognitive constraints in safety-critical and sensitivity-aware settings.

Example Coherence State

The following illustrates a stable, high-coherence interaction scenario computed using HER2NI metrics:

  • Cs ≈ 0.83 — stable human cognitive state
  • Ss ≈ 0.91 — deep but stable model reasoning load
  • Hs ≈ 0.88 — strong cross-substrate resonance

These values are static examples for interpretive clarity. In a deployed system, HER2NI metrics are computed from real interaction telemetry to evaluate stability, drift-resistance, and coherence alignment between human and AI agents.
